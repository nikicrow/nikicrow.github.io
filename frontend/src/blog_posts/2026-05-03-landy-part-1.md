Seven weeks ago I had another baby - Imogen, or Immy for short. This time I know what I am dealing with when it comes to newborns, so it's been really quite easy SO FAR **KNOCK ON WOOD**. Thing that has not been as straightforward is this time I also have a two-and-a-half-year-old who has been home with me every single day since, because we didn't want her bringing daycare germs home to a newborn immune system.

So here I am. Newborn on one arm, choas incarnate climbing the furniture with the other, trying to figure out how to keep Ember's little brain growing and stimulated while running on three hours of broken sleep. No daycare structure, no routine, no backup — just me, two kids, and a very long to-do list that I had absolutely no capacity to action.

Enter **Landy**.

---

## What is Landy?

Landy is a learning and development bot my husband and I built specifically for our situation. The name comes from **L**earning **and** **D**evelopment — because that's exactly what it does.

The idea was my husband's (he's deeply passionate about using AI effectively, which, lucky for me because he helps me a lot and knows all the free tiers lol). The core of it is this: instead of me trying to plan Ember's learning activities from scratch every day — what if we had an AI that _knew_ Ember, knew where she was at developmentally, and could just... tell me what to do that day?

And not in a complicated, prep-required way. In a **"you have two minutes and no spare hands, here's what you can do right now"** kind of way.

---

## What does it actually do?

Every day I get a message from Landy and they'll suggest activities tailored to where Ember is at. The suggestions come in two flavours:

- **Survival mode** — a two-minute activity, zero prep, can be done while holding a baby. Think: counting the stairs as we walk up them, or naming colours on whatever she's already playing with.
- **Normal mode** (I guess when Immy actually naps as she is meant to) — something more intentional. For example, Ember is working on emotional regulation, so Landy might suggest we get her favourite toys involved in a little scenario about feelings and what to do when we're frustrated.

But here's what makes it actually useful rather than just another generic activity suggestion app: **Landy knows Ember specifically**.

We've set it up with goals that are custom to where Ember is at right now:

- **Toileting independence** — she's almost there, but we're working on that last step: pulling her own pants up, wiping, washing her hands without being asked (v important with a newborn around).
- **Gross and fine motor skills** — from writing with a pencil to jumping with both feet, physical skills
- **Letters and numbers**
- **Japanese** — we're heading to Japan for a month, so we've been weaving in some basic greetings and phrases

Landy keeps track of all of this. It knows what we've been working on, what's going well, and what the medium-to-long-term goals are. It doesn't just give me random activities — it gives me _the next right step_ for Ember, on that day, given everything it knows.

---

## The memory piece

One of the things I'm most pleased about is that Landy has **persistent memory**. We use an Obsidian vault under the hood to keep track of everything: where Ember's goals are at, what activities we've done, what worked, what didn't, whether we even had time that day.

So Landy isn't starting from scratch every conversation. It remembers that we're getting close on toileting. It remembers that Ember loved the counting rocks activity last week. It remembers that yesterday was a write-off because the baby was cluster feeding and we got nothing done — and it adjusts accordingly.

It also keeps token costs relatively under control by only keeping really important snippets of info in it context window and having efficient search of the obsidian vault for the rest of the info we don't need immediately.

## What's coming

This is very much a living project. Right now I'm also building out a frontend for it — a proper interface where I can:

- Track our learning and development goals over time
- Log daily activities
- Give feedback on how Ember went (Was the activity realistic? Did we even get to it? Did she love it or hate it?)
- See her progress against longer-term milestones

I'm excited to share more about how it's built — the architecture is really interesting and I think it has applications well beyond toddler learning. More on that in posts to come.

For now though, I'll just say: parenting is not for the weak. I need all the help I can get.

That's what Landy does for us. And honestly? It's been a lifeline.
